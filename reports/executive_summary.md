## Top Churn Drivers (from SHAP Analysis)

1. **Month-to-month contract** — customers without long-term contracts churn at 3× the rate
2. **High monthly charges (>$80)** — strong predictor of dissatisfaction-driven churn
3. **Short tenure (<12 months)** — early churn window; onboarding failure is a key risk
4. **Fiber optic internet** — higher churn despite being premium service (service quality concern)
5. **Electronic check payment** — correlated with disengaged customers

## Business Recommendations

### Short-Term (0–30 days)
- Identify all High Risk customers and assign to dedicated retention team
- Offer 10–15% discount to high-value customers with month-to-month contracts
- Launch onboarding improvement for customers with tenure <6 months

### Medium-Term (30–90 days)
- Introduce annual contract incentive program (e.g., 2 months free)
- Investigate fiber optic service quality — address top complaint drivers
- Migrate electronic check payers to auto-pay with incentive

### Long-Term (90+ days)
- Deploy this churn model in production with monthly scoring refresh
- Build customer health score dashboard for account management team
- Implement cohort analysis to track retention campaign effectiveness
